Serton 20: Border Terror

Austrian border crossing, night, 1947

Setting

Austrian border crossing, night, 1947

Emotional Tone

Terror of being sent back, threat of Siberia, powerlessness before authority

Key Action

Austrian border guards stop refugees, threaten to return them to Hungary and Russian zone

Shot Count

5 shots total

Emotional Arc

Opens with tense illegal border crossing, sudden capture by Austrian guards, crushing announcement they're in Russian zone and must return, devastating realization of what return means - deportation to Siberia by Russians. Shows how refugees were trapped between zones with nowhere to go, at mercy of borders and bureaucrats.

Historical Context

1947 Austria was divided into American, British, French and Soviet zones. Being sent back to Russian-controlled area meant likely deportation to Siberian labor camps. Austrian border guards sometimes illegally extorted or returned refugees despite being in American zone.
