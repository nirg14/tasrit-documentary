Historical Context

After the war, many former Nazis remained in positions of authority in Austria and Germany, including medical facilities. Denazification was incomplete and superficial. Jewish refugees were often forced to interact with Germans who had participated in or supported the Holocaust, many of whom remained unrepentant. Medical screenings were mandatory for refugees - tuberculosis was rampant in DP camps. Children who survived the Holocaust carried sophisticated understanding of violence and harbored justified rage toward their persecutors, yet had to restrain themselves in occupied territory where Germans still held institutional power.
