Serton 25: Father's Return

Vienna, Austria - Rothschild Hospital, 1947

Setting

Rothschild Hospital gate and courtyard, Vienna

Emotional Tone

Continued despair turning to overwhelming joy, relief, reunion

Key Action

After a week or more of waiting, father finally arrives on truck, having been sheltered by Jewish farmer who gave the group milk and cheese

Shot Count

5 shots total

Emotional Arc

Opens with the child's continued lonely vigil, deepens through yet another false hope as a truck arrives without his father, captures the heartbreaking simile of walking away like a puppy whose owner hasn't come home, shifts dramatically to the joy of reunion when father finally arrives after a week or more, and concludes with the revelation that a Jewish farmer sheltered the group - showing human kindness amid chaos.

Historical Context

Many Jewish farmers and rural families in Austria and Hungary, who had survived the war in hiding or through other means, showed remarkable generosity to refugees passing through in 1946-1947. Despite their own limited resources, they would shelter groups for days or weeks, providing fresh milk, cheese, and safety. Communication was impossible - there were no phones or mail systems functioning reliably, so families separated during border crossings had no way to send word. This made every separation a potential permanent loss.
